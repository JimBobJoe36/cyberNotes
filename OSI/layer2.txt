LAN - Local Area Network
VLAN - Virtual Local Area Network
MAC address - Permanent unique hardware identifier (Ex. 00:1B:44:11:3A:B7)
Switches: Devices that use MAC addresses to route data frames to the correct location

Data Frame: a unit of data in OSI Layer 2 that carries info within a local Network
    > Destination MAC address
    > Source MAC address

Unicast - Direct communicate from one device to a specific device 
    > Destination MAC: Ex. 00:1B:44:11:3A:B7
BroadCast: Message sent to all devices on the Network
    > Destination MAC: FF:FF:FF:FF:FF:FF (Reserved MAC address for broadcasts)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
VLANs logically separate networks, instead of physically.
Less equipment needed for VLANs 
Trunking: Allows multiple VLANs to share a single link between switches, enabling data from VLANs on one switch to reach another
    > Switch 1 VLAN 100 can go to Switch 2 VLAN 100
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Problem: configuring large networks becomes slow and error-prone
    > Inconsistent VLAN numbering
    > Missing VLANs on some switches
    > Harder troubleshooting

Solution: VTP!
------------------------------------------------------------------------
VLAN Trunking Protocol (VTP)
------------------------------------------------------------------------
Facts:
    > Layer 2 Protocol
    > Shares VLAN information between switches
    > Ensures consistency accross a LAN
    > Only works over Trunk Links
    > Uses domains to group switches
Analogy:
    > VLAN Database Manager

How VTP Works:
    > VTP shares VLAN info by sending advertisements with this info:
        > IDs 
        > Names
        > Status
        > Configuration Revision Number (IMPORTANT)
    > Switches compare revision numbers to determine which VLAN database is the newest

Switches have three modes:
    > Server Mode:
        > Create, delete, modify VLANs
        > Sends and recieves updates
        > Most powerful (and dangerous)
    > Client Mode
        > Cannot create/delete VLANs
        > Recieves update
    > Transparent Mode:
        > Does NOT participate in VTP
        > Fowards advertisements only
        > VLANs are local to these switches 

What happens after a change?
    > The revision number increases
    > Other switches accept the VLAN database with the highest revision number 

How can this be dangerous?
    > A rogue or misconfigured switch can rewrite VLANs 
    > Could be accessed by a mistake trunk port 

How to prevent disasters:
    > Best Practice:
        > Use VTP Transparent Mode (Turn off VTP, essentially)
        > Reset revision # before adding new switches
        > Disable unnecessary trunk ports
        > Set VTP passwords
    > Few modern networks use VTP because of the risk.

------------------------------------------------------------------------
MAC Address Table
------------------------------------------------------------------------
> A database used by switches to map MAC addresses to specific switch ports
> Helps the switch decide where to foward frames 
> A switch can learn MAC addresses two ways: statically or dynamically

Redundancy:
    > Cons:
        > Messages are broadcasted throughout the network 
        > Later broadcasts overwrite previous ones (Switch 1 sends a MAC to Switch 2 and Switch 3, Switch 2 fowards it to Switch 3 and overwrites the original path)
        > Duplicate frames (Due to longer routes)

        > Can be fixed by a "main switch", routing messages through that switch; cuts of other routes
        
        Spanning Tree Protocol (STP):
            > Disables redundant links to break the loop
            > Keeps one path active while blockng others
            > If the main path fails, STP unblocks another
            > Enables redundancy for fault tolerance

            > STP is evil. It wants to cut your bandwith in half.
            > STP is necessary
                > Exists to prevent loops
                > Being protected is worth the cost 
                > Stability and predictability is better than speed

Port States:
    > Disabled: A port that is shutdown
    > Blocking: A port that is blocked by STP
    > Listening: Not fowarding traffic or learning MAC addresses
    > Learnning: Not fowarding, but learning MAC addresses
    > Fowarding: A normal port
