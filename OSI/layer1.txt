OSI Model
-------------
> Open Systems Interconnection
> Standardizes how systems interact with each other
> Hardware covers layers 1-3
> Layer 4 is Transmission
> Software covers layers 4-7
-------------
Layer 1: The Physical Layer
-------------
> Hardware, not software
    > Cables, network adapters, and wireless signals
    > not software
> Bits, not bites
    > Data travels through cables through 0s and 1s
    > Could be radio, light, or electric signals
> Bit Rate Control
    > Determines speed of transmission (bits/second)

TYPES
> Copper Cables (Cat5e, Cat6, Cat6a)
    > Transmits data via electrical signals

> Fiber Optic (Single or Multi mode)
    > Uses light pulses for ultra-fast transmission

> Wireless (Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, Cellular)
    > Transmits data through radio waves

> Speed: measures in Mbps or Gbps
> Duplex: Half-Duplex (one direction at a time) or Full-Duplex (both directions simultaneously)
> Basic Errors: Signal degradation, collisions, and interference can corrupt data transmission

PHYSICAL THREATS
> Electromatic Interference (EMI): External electrical signals disrupt data flow
> Crosstalk: Signal leakage between cables
> Signal Degradation: Loss of signal strength over distance
> Environmental Threats: Threats from nature, such as floods, fires, and tornadoes. Can also just be corrosion

CABLE-BASED ATTACKS
> Passive interception: Monitors cable emissions without detections
> Active Manipulation: Injecting data, modifying traffic, or man-in-the-middle ATTACKS
